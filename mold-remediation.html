Mold Remediation

What Is Mold?

Mold is a surface growth that can be visible or hidden from sight. It's a living organism that grows by extending thread like strands (hyphae) and replicates by shedding spores. Molds can be very harmful or just an irritation. Of course people react differently to mold spores. Regardless of the effect - mild to fatal - molds that grow in our homes indicate a presence of water and a food source. Unfortunately, drywall and soft woods or glued boards are a food source. Water can come from leaks from roofs, supply lines, exterior wall claddings especially the old pressed boards, drips, condensation especially from AC lines in the attic, overflows from toilets, showers, sinks and drip pans, or even just high humidity! Some symptoms of mold growth include:

  • Watery Eyes
  • Runny or Stuffy Nose
  • Itchy eyes, nose, and throat
  • Sneezing or Coughing
  • Irritation of the Skin

How Quickly Can Mold Start Growing?

Mold hygenist John Sang CMAC IAQS reported mold can start within 48hours especially if the water carried contaminates such as a sewer backup. Clean water from a supply line should not carry contaminates but after a few days it can pick up contaminates from building materials that cause mold to start growing. Water damage brings with it increased humidity as well as contaminates.

That’s why immediate action is needed to start drying and removing or lifting wet materials to enable proper drying has to start without delay. Carpet pads have to be lifted to enable carpets and floor surfaces to dry. Wet drywall has to be removed as water can wick up. Soft baseboards are often made from offcuts glued together or from man made fibrous materials that wick water inside if its damaged. Cabinetry is our biggest concern. Particle board cabinets soak up water and puff out quickly. They are rarely salvageable. Solid wood cabinets might be salvageable depending on materials and finishes. But, cat 3 water spills from sewer backups or flood water carries contaminates that require removal immediately. The drywall behind water affected cabinets still has to be dried out to prevent hidden mold from growing.


Our Process

Our certified inspectors will provide a ballpark estimate range based on their experience and extent of the known damage. However, mold frequently catches us all out! We have suspected part of a wall, not seen additional ater sign, but ended up seeing mold that has grown organically from humidity extend to much larger areas. With mold no one can tell you exactly how much the final cost will be until the edges of the mold spread are found. Even mold hygienists in their protocols for remediation always direct to remove all affected surfaces plus 12’’ as they don’t know the extent of hidden mold. But, all our removal and treatment work has to be documented whether its for you as a homeowner or for an insurance carrier.   2. Set negative air pressure machines to HEPA filter the air of spores while work progresses. Set drying equipment and dehumidifier to remove excess humidity. Equipment set is per IICRC standards and approved by insurance adjuster.
  3. Mold removed by cutting out and bagging all mold affected materials. Removal of cabinets approved by adjuster or homeowner depending on likely hidden water and mold behind units.
  4. Anti-microbial treatments applied to kill any mold spores.
  5. Extra treatments depend on hygenist protocols may include encapsulation or stud cleaning. This process should take about five days for the typical localized mitigation. However, whole house damage will take longer. Estimates account for the actual amount of work done, materials removed, equipment used and treatments completed. Mitigation is always separate from repairs or Reconstruction of areas.
