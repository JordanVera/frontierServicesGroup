Structure/Smoke Cleaning

What Is Structure Cleaning?

Smoke is a labor intensive process that requires a team of technicians to use special Chem Sponges to manually test and clean the surfaces of smoke residue. Popcorn ceilings are special concern as they can’t be effectively cleaned without damage to the popcorn finish. Then portable ozone machines can be set to fill the home with ozone that kills spores and neutralizes odors. The property has to be vacated during ozone treatment process and checked prior to reentry.

With smoke damaged properties HVAC duct cleaning is required if the AC system was on at the time of the fire. If the ducts are not cleaned then smoke particles will blow out of registers as soon the system is turned on.


When Is Structure Cleaning Needed?

SCS can be required following smoke damage from a fire or from advanced mold after water damage especially flood

  1. Smoke from a fire leaves a residue which can be oily or a carbon residue. Smoke gets into AC ducts and equipment, appliances, under materials, behind cabinets and all over your personal contents. Clean up after smoke damage from a fire is a methodical process to manually clean all surfaces and often to treat smoke affected areas with an ozone machine.
  2. The second application for structure clean is following water damage especially from a flood that brings contaminates in from the outside or up from the sewers.
